# Methodology

## Purpose

This repository documents a live digital reconstruction of the BMI project drawings associated with Cedric Price.

The work originates in published material from *Cedric Price Supplement No. 3* and related sources, including *Architectural Design* 6/71 and other archival references. No scans or direct reproductions of publication pages are included in this repository.

Instead, the repository contains original SVG reconstructions and interpretive facsimiles produced as part of an ongoing research study.

---

## Research approach

The project treats drawing reconstruction as a method of architectural inquiry.

Rather than simply reproducing historical drawings, the work investigates how architecture may be understood as a process of progression through:

* iterative reconstruction
* version control
* comparative redrawing
* methodological reflection
* visible stages of development

The repository therefore functions as both:

1. a collection of completed SVG drawings
2. a live record of how those drawings are produced

---

## Drawing workflow

Each drawing progresses through the following stages:

### Working

Initial linework, rough tracing, and spatial interpretation.

### Verified

Drawing reviewed against source material and corrected where necessary.

### Published

Stable version exported for use in the Miraheze wiki and research outputs.

---

## File naming

Standard file syntax:

`cp-bmi-###-drawing-title-v##.svg`

Example:

`cp-bmi-001-overall-plan-v01.svg`

---

## Research significance

This repository is intended as a live demonstration of progression in the spirit of Cedric Price’s own working methods, in which architecture is understood as iterative, open-ended, and continuously developing.
